Serial dictator program

I wrote this program to help with assigning papers to reviewers for a peer review group I helped start. To use it, just upload a CSV that contains an identifier and a ranked list. The program is written in the wonderful Julia language, but I am more than happy to write similar programs in other languages (most easily in R or Stata) if there is a demand!

The serial dictator mechanism is used. To illustrate, assume there are three papers to be assigned \(\{a,b,c\}\) and three potential reviewers \(\{1,2,3\}\). Suppose reviewer \(i\) has preferences \(P_i\).

\[ \begin{array}{rl} P_1: & a - b - c \\ P_2: & a - c - b \\ P_3: & b - a - c \end{array} \]

We will consider all possible orders of dictatorship:

\[ \begin{array}{rl} \text{order 1}: & 1 \to 2 \to 3 \\ \text{order 2}: & 1 \to 3 \to 2 \\ \text{order 3}: & 2 \to 1 \to 3 \\ \text{order 4}: & 2 \to 3 \to 1 \\ \text{order 5}: & 3 \to 1 \to 2 \\ \text{order 6}: & 3 \to 2 \to 1 \end{array} \]

Consider order 1. The allocation for dictator order 1 for individual \(i\) is denoted \(\mu_1(i)\).

  1. Define the set of available objects as \(A_1 = \{1,2,3\}\)
  2. Person 1 chooses first. All objects are in the available set, so she gets her most preferred item. So, \(\mu_1(1) = a\). Update the set of available objects to be \(A_2 = A_1 \setminus \{\mu_1(1)\}\).
  3. Person 2 chooses her most preferred item from the set of available objects, \(A_2\). Her most preferred item is not available, so she gets her second-most preferred item. So, \(\mu_1(2) = c\). Update the set of available objects to be \(A_3 = A_2 \setminus \{\mu_1(1), \mu_1(2)\}\).
  4. There is only one item available, so the third person is assigned that item. So \(\mu_1(3) = b\).

The final allocation for dictator order 1 is \(\mu = (a,c,b)\). How well of are the people under this allocation? A simple way to calculate welfare is to subtract 4 from the ranking of the allocation. Let \(P_i(\mu_1)\) be the ranking of person \(i\)’s allocated object, their payoff is then \(4 - P_i(\mu_1)\). The total welfare of the allocation for serial dictator order \(k\) is \[V_k = \sum_{i=1}^3 \left(4 - P_i(\mu_k)\right).\] The payoff to order 1, then, is 5.
